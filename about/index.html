Mike Jeter's Lifelong Connection with Dogs in Spring, Texas

Mike Jeter grew up in Spring, Texas, and has been training dogs professionally for over 20 years. As a graduate of Klein Oak High School and lifelong outdoorsman, Mike developed his natural talent for working with dogs while serving as a kennel technician and assistant trainer during college at Lone Star College. His deep roots in the Spring community make him uniquely qualified to serve local dog owners with personalized, professional training services.

What started as a part-time job during school quickly revealed itself as Mike's true calling. Local kennel owners recognized his unique ability to connect with dogs of all breeds, sizes, and temperaments—and his gift for bringing out the best in even the most challenging cases.

More Than Just a Dog Trainer in Spring, Texas

Mike's extensive experience includes training in obedience, behavior modification, hunt and field training for retrievers, and Schutzhund-style training. Having successfully trained thousands of dogs locally, K-9 Manners of Texas has become the “go-to” name among veterinarians when other training attempts have failed.

At the start of his career, Mike gained valuable insight working as a veterinarian technician, learning to discern early clues about a dog's physical health and how health conditions might affect training and behavior.

As both an avid hunter and dog lover, Mike understands the unique needs of working dogs and family pets alike. He has enjoyed breeding American Staffordshire Terriers and now focuses on breeding top-notch field Labradors with excellent household pet temperaments.

Mike Jeter's Family-Centered Dog Training Approach

Mike has two wonderful children who love helping out at times—hugging, walking, relating to, or just being present as part of the “distraction” in a real-world training setting.

Graduation time includes teaching the trainee's whole family, including the children, in their role in the dog's successful behavior within the family and community. This ensures everyone knows how to maintain the training and the dog responds to all family members.

For over 20 years, Mike has developed trusted relationships with dogs and their families through achieving desired training results. Many clients are now on their second or third generation of companion pets and hunting dogs with K-9 Manners.

How long has Mike Jeter been training dogs in Spring, Texas?
